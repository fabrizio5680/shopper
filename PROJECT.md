# Shopper — User Journey

## What is Shopper?

Shopper helps you plan meals and instantly compare ingredient prices across 5 Irish supermarkets — Tesco, SuperValu, Dunnes, Aldi, and Lidl. Tell it what you want to cook, and it builds your shopping list and finds the best prices automatically.

---

## Getting Started

You land on a welcome page. Sign in with your Google account — one click, no form to fill in. Once signed in, you're taken to your dashboard.

---

## Building a Recipe Search

### 1. Name your recipe

Type in what you'd like to cook — "Ramen", "Pasta Carbonara", "Beef Stew". This is the only free-text field; everything else is structured to keep suggestions focused and relevant.

### 2. Refine with dietary preferences (optional)

Pick any combination of preference chips that apply to you:

- **Dietary**: Gluten-free, Dairy-free, Vegan, Vegetarian, Pescatarian, Halal, Kosher
- **Quality**: Organic, Non-processed, Whole foods, Free-range, Grass-fed
- **Health**: Low-sodium, Low-sugar, High-protein, Low-carb, High-fibre, Low-fat
- **Lifestyle**: Budget-friendly, Easy to find, Seasonal, Local produce

### 3. Add keyword refinements (optional)

Add up to 5 short tags to nudge the suggestions — e.g. "chicken broth", "spicy", "quick". Press Enter or comma after each one.

### 4. Submit

Hit the button. The AI analyses your recipe with all your preferences taken into account and suggests a shopping list in a few seconds.

---

## Reviewing Ingredients

Before anything is saved, you see the full list of suggested ingredients and can review them:

- Each ingredient shows its name, quantity, and any relevant notes (e.g. "lactose-free preferred")
- Each ingredient also shows a quick-link to search for it on each of the 5 supermarkets — so you can check what's actually available before committing
- Tick the ingredients you want to keep — nothing is selected by default
- Use "Select all / Deselect all" to adjust quickly
- Hit **Save ingredients** when you're happy — only the ticked ones are kept

If you change your mind entirely, hit **Cancel** — the search is discarded cleanly.

---

## Finding Prices

Once you save your ingredients, Shopper searches all 5 supermarkets simultaneously. You'll see each ingredient's status update in real time as results come in.

When done, each ingredient shows a tab for every supermarket with matching products — name, price, unit price, and a direct link to buy. If a supermarket has nothing, that tab shows "Not found" clearly.

---

## Adding More Ingredients

You have two ways to add ingredients after the initial list is saved:

### Rescan with AI

On your recipe page, hit **Rescan for more ingredients**. The AI looks at what's already on your list and suggests only ingredients that aren't there yet. You review and approve them the same way as before.

### Search manually

Use the manual search bar at the bottom of your recipe page. Type any ingredient — "whole milk", "sourdough bread", "olive oil" — and Shopper searches the supermarkets immediately.

You see the results laid out by supermarket, with each product shown as a card. Pick the specific products you want to keep, give the ingredient a name (pre-filled from your search), and optionally add a quantity or note. Hit **Save ingredient** and it's added to your list instantly alongside everything else.

---

## Managing Your Searches

Every recipe submission is saved as a separate search on your dashboard — you can run the same recipe multiple times with different preferences and compare results side by side.

Each card on the dashboard shows:

- Recipe name and the preferences you used
- How many ingredients and products were found
- Status (searching / done / error)
- When it was created

You can delete any search at any time. A confirmation prompt appears first — deleting removes the search, all its ingredients, and all product results permanently.

---

## The Full Loop

1. Sign in
2. Name a recipe, pick preferences, add keywords
3. Review and approve suggested ingredients
4. Watch prices load across 5 supermarkets
5. Add more ingredients via AI rescan or manual search
6. Click through to buy directly on any supermarket's site
7. Run the same recipe again with different preferences to compare
8. Delete searches you no longer need
